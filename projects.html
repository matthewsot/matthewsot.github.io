I focus on a wide variety of problems that tend to either solve a legitimate problem in the lives of those around me or which I expect to be able to learn a lot from solving.

My current interests include computational neuroscience, data exploration, synthetic biology, genetics, and education.

  • SyReNN - a neural network analysis tool based on our research (eg. [1], [2]) on computing linear restrictions of piecewise-linear neural networks.
  • ISAM - a novel compiler approach for deep learning hardware capable of producing code up to 3-5X faster than hand-optimized libraries.
  • DeepThin - a novel neural network compression method and library achieving 60% better accuracy and up to 15X faster inference speeds.
  • Action - a browser add-on that simplifies tracking action items during meetings. Used by teams at Google, Microsoft, U.C. Berkeley, and more. Currently in long-term-support mode for a small number of users, no longer available to new teams.
  • Docs+ - the most powerful third-party library for building Google Docs addons
  • LHSCampaign - online platform used by all student office candidates at Lynbrook High School. LHSCampaign was recently acquired by Lynbrook, and serves over 1,700 unique users each year.
  • Develop Academy - now in its second year, Develop is a middle school summer camp that teaches skills often overlooked by traditional schooling.
  • Classfinder - a website students can use to share schedules and find out who will be in their classes. Used by over 700 students across four high schools each year.
  • SharpSwift - uses Microsoft's Roslyn compiler platform to translate C# code to Swift.

You can view more of my projects (and their code) on my Github profile.
